## Client Pitch

**Project:** Preventing SLF Contamination in Grape Harvests  
**Team:** Grape Soup  
**Client(s):** Cornell CALS Extension / E&J Gallo Winery / National Grape

### Problem statement
Spotted lanternflies (SLFs) can contaminate mechanically harvested grapes when insects on vines are collected along with fruit, creating a sorting/separation problem before downstream processing. USDA reports ~923,000 bearing acres of grapes and 5.40 million tons of utilized grape production (2024) in comparable states, and SLF populations are reported in 19 states plus D.C. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

### Proposed direction
We propose a density-based separation system at the receiving area: solids enter a water-filled flotation tank where grapes sink and SLFs float, then a mechanical surface skimmer removes floaters. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

### Key risks / unknowns
Performance with stems/debris and damaged fruit, potential fruit-quality impacts from water exposure, and scalability to debris-heavy field conditions. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Functional Prototype

### What we built
A crank-driven paddlewheel skimmer mounted above a flotation tank. CAD parts were delayed, so a wood/cardboard build matching planned geometry was used to complete early mechanical tests. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

### How it works / how it’s used
Floaters remain at the surface and are pushed toward an exit/discard region by a rotating paddlewheel, while grapes remain below the surface. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

### Design tests (summary)
- **Motion:** Paddle rotated 1:1 with crank for 10 revolutions with no binding. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}  
- **Interference:** No collisions between paddles and tank/brackets over 10 revolutions (care required due to taped wood brackets). :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}  
- **Fastening/stability:** Some bracket translation observed; next iteration targets tighter mounting and reduced shaft play. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}  
- **Repeat-use cycling:** ~60 rpm for 1 minute with no visible cracking/loosening. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

## Client Report

### Proposed solution and prototype
We developed a density-based separation system for the receiving area: after initial liquid/solid separation, solids enter a water-filled flotation tank where grapes sink and SLFs float; a rotary paddle mechanism skims floaters from the surface while clean grapes drain from the bottom to continue processing. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

### Key testing results
- Low speed (~8.3 rpm): 4–6 out of 6 removals in 40–60 s (~83% avg). :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}  
- High speed (~16.5 rpm): 5 out of 6 removals in ~20 s (~83%). :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}  
- Observed grape loss: 0%; grapes-only controls transferred 0 grapes to discard. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}  

### Conclusion and next steps
Plain water was sufficient for separation (no sugar/density modifiers needed), helping keep the process stream “clean” and reducing added residue/cleanup. Next steps include adding a dedicated disposal/drain valve for controlled disposal and easier water-level maintenance during longer runs, especially at higher rpm. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
